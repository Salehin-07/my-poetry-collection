Seven Poems

01

The Thoughts of a Thought

A philosophical journey between the thought and the writer

A metaphysical dialogue exploring the eternal question: Hero or villain? This poem unravels the paradox of impossible choices.

A thought is just a fiction. It's nothing to worry about.

02

Law — Unnecessary or Unused

A philosophical dialogue on the nature of law

A traveler meets a wise old man who reveals a profound truth about law: whether it saves the good or punishes the bad.

Law is a necessary decoration that's never used.
Read
03

A Late Return or Not

Where the heart becomes the courtroom

The mind is a court, two egos are lawyers, and the question of love stands as defendant.

The best thing at first may become the worst at last.
Read
04

Seas of Past

A reflection on time, age, and the ironic cycles of life

Travel through the seas of time—from old age to youth to childhood—and witness the beautiful irony of human desire.

The cycle of life is just like a joke that no one laughs at.
Read
05

The Staircase of Wisdom

Life's stages divided by wisdom

The more one knows, the more humble knowledge makes him. A journey through the three steps of life's staircase.

Life is the staircase of three steps, divided by wisdom.
Read
06

Unknown Crimson

A love poem to a muse never met

Under the crimson moon, love blooms for someone who exists beyond reality—a mysterious figure who lives in the mind and heart.

She was the one I never met, nor do I know her name.
Read
07

The Crimson of Shining Gold and Mud

Two paths, one search, an eternal question

A traveler walks two roads—one of shining gold, one of humble mud—searching for the crimson of true humanity, only to find it may never be in the path itself.

But the crimson I wanted to find, shouldn't it be in either the gold or the mud?

About the Poet

Md Abu Salehin

I am a writer who believes in the transformative power of poetry. Through my verses, I explore the complexities of human emotion, the absurdities of existence, and the quiet beauty of fleeting moments.

Poetry, to me, is both a mirror and a window—a way to see myself more clearly while also offering others a glimpse into the universal experiences that connect us all.

Each poem here is a fragment of my inner world, crafted with vulnerability and honesty. I invite you to wander through these words, to feel what I felt, and perhaps discover something about yourself along the way.
